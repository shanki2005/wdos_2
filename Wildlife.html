Our Wildlife And Where To Find Them

"Sri Lanka's wildlife invites you to witness the beauty of its untamed landscapes, fostering a deep appreciation for the delicate balance between nature and the diverse species that call this island home."

Sinharaja Forrest Reserve

Sinharaja, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a lush tropical rainforest that stands as a biodiversity hotspot. Home to a myriad of endemic species, including the Purple-faced Langur and the Ceylon Magpie, it offers a unique opportunity to explore an undisturbed natural habitat.

Horton Plains National Park

Amidst the central highlands, Horton Plains is characterized by rolling hills, misty landscapes, and diverse ecosystems. The park is known for its iconic World's End, offering breathtaking views. Visitors can encounter wildlife such as the Sambar Deer and explore the enchanting Baker's Falls.
